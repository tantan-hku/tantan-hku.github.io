About

Group Lead

Dr. Tan Tan is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Real Estate and Construction, Faculty of Architecture, The University of Hong Kong (HKU), Program Director of MSc Digital Management of Built Assets (DMBA), and a Principal Investigator at the National Centre of Technology Innovation for Digital Construction in Hong Kong.

  • Training: PhD and MSc from UCL Bartlett; BArch from Huazhong University of Science and Technology.
  • Research: Design Engineering Informatics, Design for Excellence, DfMA, Design for Safety, and digital construction management.
  • Funding: Projects supported by RGC, NSFC, URC, CRF, ITF, and HKU schemes.
  • Service: Managing guest editor for Developments in the Built Environment, Corresponding Expert for Frontiers of Engineering Management, and Co-Chair for CAAD Futures 2025.

Research Focus

Design Decisions Shape Construction Outcomes

Construction contributes nearly 37% of global carbon emissions and faces severe safety risks. DxDC studies how design-stage intelligence can reduce lifecycle cost, environmental impact, and safety risk before they are locked into projects.

01

Human-AI Collaboration in Generative AI

Studying knowledge co-creation, cognition, creativity, and changing professional roles in AI-supported engineering design.

02

LLM-assisted Design Checking

Developing intelligent assistants for interpreting building codes, drawings, BIM data, and alteration and addition works.

03

Generative Design for Modular Construction

Integrating algorithmic design with manufacturing, transportation, lifting, cost, and aesthetic constraints.
