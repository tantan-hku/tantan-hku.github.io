Design for Safety

Designing risk out before it reaches the construction site.

Construction safety is often treated as a site-management problem, yet many hazards are already shaped when drawings, sequences, interfaces, and construction methods are still being designed. Our DfS work asks how digital design intelligence can make safer work the default condition.

Why It Matters

Safety Begins Upstream

Falls from height, electrocutions, caught-between incidents, struck-by-object injuries, and overexertion remain persistent construction risks. Regulations and site procedures are essential, but they often arrive after key design and planning decisions have already defined the work environment.

DfS reframes safety as a design responsibility: identifying foreseeable hazards, comparing safer alternatives, and embedding construction knowledge into the decisions that shape how a building will be made.
