Designing risk out before it reaches the construction site.

Construction safety is often treated as a site-management problem, yet many hazards are already shaped when drawings, sequences, interfaces, and construction methods are still being designed.

Safety Begins Upstream

DfS reframes safety as a design responsibility: identify foreseeable hazards, compare safer alternatives, and embed construction knowledge into early design decisions.

Falls from height, electrocutions, caught-between incidents, struck-by-object injuries, and overexertion remain persistent construction risks. Regulations and site procedures are essential, but they often arrive after key design and planning decisions have already defined the work environment.

Our research connects design information, construction methods, hazard knowledge, and human expertise. We explore how BIM, knowledge graphs, large language models, and human-AI collaboration can support earlier recognition of construction risk.
