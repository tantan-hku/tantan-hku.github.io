Research Direction 02 · DfMA

Design for Manufacture and Assembly

Moving construction from fragmented site work toward productized, manufacturable building systems.

DfMA treats buildings as assemblies shaped by production, logistics, tolerance, sequencing, and lifecycle value. It asks how design can make construction more predictable, safer, cleaner, and easier to scale.

Why It Matters

Construction Needs a Production Logic

Traditional construction relies heavily on fragmented on-site work, variable labour conditions, rework, waste, and late coordination. These problems are not only site problems; they are often symptoms of designs that were not prepared for manufacture, transport, and assembly.

DfMA moves construction thinking upstream. It links architectural intent with modules, interfaces, supply chains, factory capability, craneage, sequencing, carbon, cost, and end-of-life adaptability.
